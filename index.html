How it works

Three steps to a quieter mind

1
Capture what's open
Write down anything unfinished — tasks, decisions, conversations, worries. One thought at a time, as it arrives.
2
Clarify what it needs
Every loop gets a type: Do, Decide, Ask, Schedule, or Let go. Naming the kind of attention is what makes it lighter.
3
Process it with intention
Review your loops one at a time, update the next move, and release what is no longer yours to carry.
Five kinds of open loop
Do
A concrete action you can start right now without extra planning.
Decide
A choice you are holding open. Name it cleanly and stop circling it.
Ask
One person, one ask. Something you need from someone else.
Schedule
Something that needs a real time on the calendar to move forward.
Let go
Choosing to release it. The goal is relief, not proving it was important.
