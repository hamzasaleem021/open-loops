Open Loops v1 walkthrough

Calm clarity, one unfinished thing at a time.

This is a clickable visual prototype for the Open Loops concept: capture a thought, make it clearer in three calm steps, and return to a home screen that stays gentle even when life does not.

Thesis

A personal clarity tool, not a task manager. It helps unresolved thoughts land somewhere calm.

Audience posture

Quietly universal and AuDHD-friendly: low-overwhelm, low-guilt, guided without feeling clinical.

Core loop

Re-entry - capture - clarify - now. The app keeps showing only a few things that matter right now.

Device
Walkthrough step
Use this prototype

Switch devices, move through the flow, or click inside the preview itself. On laptop, select a loop card to reveal the optional detail pane.

Design rules
One primary action Every screen has one obvious next move and one softer escape hatch.
Not the full backlog Home surfaces only a few active loops, plus a gentle re-entry card.
No guilt mechanics No streaks, no overdue states, no red urgency, no failure language.
Clarify, do not manage The product turns vague residue into a clear next state. It does not become a system of everything.
Home Re-entry card, calm now list, and a bottom capture action.
Current posture Samsung A56
Why it feels calmer

It does not make everything equally loud.

The prototype keeps the main screen narrow in meaning, not just narrow in width. Now is visible, Waiting is collapsed, Released is tucked away, and Inbox only appears if something still needs clarification.

Responsive behavior

The flow adapts without becoming a dashboard.

  • Phone stays single-column with a bottom-fixed capture button.
  • Tablet breathes wider and lets Waiting open as a slide-over, not a second column.
  • Laptop keeps a centered reading-width layout and only shows a detail pane after selection.
Intentionally absent

No cleverness that raises the temperature.

  • No dense dashboards, badges, nested projects, or kanban behavior.
  • No guilt language like overdue, missed, behind, or failing to keep up.
  • No AI coaching or life-optimization framing in this first pass.
